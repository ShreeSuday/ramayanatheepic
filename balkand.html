The Bal Kand

Bala Kanda is the first book of the Ramayana, describing the birth and early life of Lord Rama. It narrates the story of King Dasharatha’s yajna to obtain sons, resulting in the divine birth of Rama, Lakshmana, Bharata, and Shatrughna. Sage Vishwamitra takes young Rama and Lakshmana to the forest to protect his yajna from demons, where Rama slays Tataka and receives divine weapons. They then visit Mithila, where Rama breaks Lord Shiva’s bow in a swayamvara, winning the hand of Sita. The kanda ends with their grand wedding, marking the beginning of Rama’s divine journey.
